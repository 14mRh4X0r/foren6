        <div class="column1-wrap">
            <div class="column1"><p class="poneliner"><strong>Visualize your 6LoWPAN network</strong>. Foren6 uses sniffers to capture 6LoWPAN traffic and renders the network state it in a graphical user interface.</p></div>
        </div>
        <div class="column2"><p class="poneliner"><strong>Customize to your infrastructure</strong>. The network viewer uses floating positions, or user-defined layouts to visualize sensors in their real setting.</p></div>
        <div class="clear"></div>
     </section>
      <section class="soneliner">
        <div class="column1-wrap">
            <div class="column1"><p class="poneliner"><strong>On-site diagnosis</strong>. Foren6 captures live packets from deployed networks in a non-intrusive manner. Multiple sniffers can be combined for extended coverage.</p></div>
        </div>
        <div class="column2"><p class="poneliner"><strong>Multiple source types</strong>. Multiple sniffer types are supported. You can even import existing PCAP files for a post-mortem analysis or connect to a live COOJA simulation.</p></div>
        <div class="clear"></div>
     </section>
     
      <section class="soneliner">
        <div class="column1-wrap">
            <div class="column1"><p class="poneliner"><strong>Debug-oriented</strong>. Rewind the packet capture history, replay a previous packet trace and navigate through different overlays to pinpoint problems.</p></div>
        </div>
        <div class="column2"><p class="poneliner"><strong>Detect routing problems</strong>. The Routing Protocol for 6LoWPAN Networks, RPL, is an emerging IETF standard. Foren6 captures all RPL-related information and is capable of identifiying abnormal behaviors.</p></div>
        <div class="clear"></div>
      </section>
      <section class="soneliner">
        <div class="column1-wrap">
            <div class="column1"><p class="poneliner"><strong>Android support</strong>. An Android port is under development, allowing to visualize 6LoWPAN networks on a tablet. It is ideal for walking around in a WSN.</p></div>
        </div>
        <div class="column2"><p class="poneliner"></p><strong>Free & Open Source</strong>. Start using Foren6 today at no cost. The open-source community around the project will help it grow and acquire additional features.</div>
